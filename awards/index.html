About the Awards

The objective of The Harvard Crimson’s Journalism Awards program is to recognize superb journalism produced at the high school level. We will be taking entries from nearly all aspects of journalism, with categories including general news, features, sports and arts, opinion, and overall reporter, along with multimedia such as photo, video, and newspaper design. Given the rise of digital journalism, online publications are also encouraged to enter via a dedicated award category. All entries are eligible to receive comments and critique, as well as access to grading based on our proprietary internally-developed rubric.

Please sign up under the “Subscribe & Contact” page to receive more information as the entry period and deadlines are finalized. We look forward to reviewing your entry.

About The Crimson

The Harvard Crimson, the nation's oldest continuously published daily college newspaper, was founded in 1873 and incorporated in 1967. The Crimson has a rich tradition of journalistic integrity and counts among its ranks of editorship some of America's greatest journalists. The faces of Pulitzer Prize-winning Crimson editors line the walls of The Crimson.

The Crimson is proud of its legacy of alumni active in journalism, business, public service, and politics. Past editors include Franklin D. Roosevelt, Class of 1904, John F. Kennedy '40, J. Anthony Lewis '48, Don Graham '65, Linda Greenhouse '68, Steve Ballmer '77, Jim Cramer '77, Susan Chira '80, and Jeff Zucker '86. Having grown from a fortnightly newspaper to a daily, 140 years after its founding The Crimson continues to flourish.
